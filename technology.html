The Technology

Together with NASA, a novel radiation shielding has been developed, to overcome radiation challenges for spacecraft equipment and other electronic components affected by ionising radiation. Most radiation shielding we have today are developed based on Aluminium based composites. However, these novel state-of-the art shielding advanced materials are based on the atomic number Z.

Limitations of Aluminium Shielding

The current commercial Al wall shielding thickness being offered in the CubeSat commercial market offers basically no electron radiation protection for polar LEO or GEO. The Z-shielding light is the thinnest and lightest of the Z-shields. It performs 100 times better over Al with respect to thickness. Excellent choice for higher altitude LEO orbiting spacecraft.  Greater electron radiation shielding than Z-Shields LEO-Light.  

Overall benefits of the Z-shielding Technology

  1. Extends typical CubeSat missions from 3 months to years with an atomic number (Z)-grade vault. 
  2. Demonstrates a Charge Dissipation Film designed for extreme charging environments.
  3. Develops and demonstrates a one-piece (Z)-grade radiation protection for electron radiation environments.
  4. Reduces technology development schedule and associated costs by collective testing in a relevant space
